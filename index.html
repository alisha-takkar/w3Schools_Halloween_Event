Symbols

There are several symbols and artefacts associated with Halloween.

Jack-o'-lanterns are traditionally carried by guisers on All Hallows' Eve in order to frighten evil spirits. There is a popular Irish Christian folktale associated with the jack-o'-lantern, which in folklore is said to represent a "soul who has been denied entry into both heaven and hell".

Another famous Halloween symbol is the bat. During the cold nights of Autumn, bats are most active, eating as much fat as possible for hibernation. Large swarms of distrated insects attracted bats and they became associated with the ceremony.

It is believed that the veil between living and dead is thinned alloweing spirits to cross over to our realm. People sing hymns and lit candles for family members that passed on. Icons of ghosts and skulls represent these traditions today.

Trick-or-treat

Trick-or-treating is a customary celebration for children on Halloween. Children go in costume from house to house, asking for treats such as candy or sometimes money, with the question, "Trick or treat?" The word "trick" implies a "threat" to perform mischief on the homeowners or their property if no treat is given.
